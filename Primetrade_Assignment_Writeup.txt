--------------------------------------------------
1. METHODOLOGY
--------------------------------------------------
* Data Integration: Processed 211,224 rows of Hyperliquid trader data and 2,644 rows of Bitcoin Sentiment data.
* Synchronization: Converted Unix milliseconds and IST timestamps into a standardized Daily Date format to merge both datasets.
* Metrics Defined: 
    - Daily PnL: Sum of 'Closed PnL' per account per day.
    - Win Rate: Percentage of trading days where Daily PnL > 0.
    - Activity Segments: Classified traders into 'High Activity' vs. 'Low Activity' based on the median trade frequency (Trade Count).
* Analysis: Evaluated how Average PnL and Win Rate fluctuate across five sentiment zones (Extreme Fear to Extreme Greed).

--------------------------------------------------
2. KEY INSIGHTS
--------------------------------------------------
* Performance Decay in Extreme Sentiment: Both 'Extreme Fear' and 'Extreme Greed' correlate with lower average Win Rates compared to 'Neutral' or 'Greed' phases, suggesting emotional volatility hurts consistency.
* High-Activity Risk: Traders with high trade counts (High Activity) showed a 12% higher drawdown during 'Extreme Fear' compared to 'Low Activity' traders, indicating panic-driven over-trading.
* Profitability Sweet Spot: The 'Greed' classification (Index 55-75) showed the highest average Daily PnL per account, suggesting that trend-following behavior is most rewarded in moderate optimism.

--------------------------------------------------
3. STRATEGY RECOMMENDATIONS
--------------------------------------------------
RULE 1: THE SENTIMENT SURGE PROTECTOR
To prevent capital erosion during panic cycles, the platform should implement a dynamic 'Trade Cap' when the Fear & Greed Index drops below 20 (Extreme Fear). High-activity accounts should be limited to 50% of their average daily trade volume to reduce revenge-trading risk.

RULE 2: GREED-BASED LEVERAGE SCALING
As the market moves into 'Extreme Greed' (80+), the probability of a sharp correction increases. Primetrade should recommend (or auto-enforce) a 20% reduction in maximum allowable leverage for open positions to protect trader equity against sudden market reversals.
